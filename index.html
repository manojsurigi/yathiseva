About Us

Yathi Sevashram is not just a spiritual sanctuary; it is a haven that offers a unique blend of divine connection, spiritual growth, and environmental consciousness. This peaceful refuge allows visitors to engage in a journey of self-discovery, learn the profound significance of nature, and find tranquility and enlightenment in every corner.

Our ashram is dedicated to fostering an environment of nurturing both spiritual and community well-being. Here, people from all walks of life come together to experience personal growth, build meaningful connections, and find peace through meditation, mindfulness, and acts of selfless service.

Whether you are seeking peace, spiritual awakening, or simply a place to connect with nature and like-minded individuals, Yathi Sevashram offers a transformative experience that will leave you rejuvenated in mind, body, and spirit.

Virat Venkateswara Swamy Temple

The Virat Venkateswara Swamy Temple is a prominent Hindu temple located in Alamkhanpally, near Kadapa, Andhra Pradesh. It is renowned for its towering 81-foot statue of Lord Venkateswara, making it a significant spiritual and architectural landmark in the region.

The temple's architecture reflects traditional South Indian design, featuring intricate carvings and a majestic gopuram (tower). The temple complex also includes a goshala (cow shelter), emphasizing its commitment to animal welfare and traditional values.

The temple is easily accessible from Kadapa city and is a must-visit for those interested in experiencing the spiritual and cultural heritage of the region.

Events & Festivals

Dhanurmasam (Margazhi Month)

Dhanurmasam, also known as the month of Margazhi, is a highly auspicious period in the Hindu calendar, dedicated to the worship of Lord Vishnu. This sacred month typically falls between mid-December and mid-January. Devotees actively participate in early morning prayers, recitations of the Thiruppavai (composed by Andal), and various devotional activities to seek divine blessings.

  • Early morning Suprabhata Seva (waking the deity)
  • Thiruppavai recitations
  • Special Alankarams (decorations) for the deities
  • Distribution of prasadam to devotees

Varshikotsavam (Annual Temple Festival)

Varshikotsavam is the grand annual temple festival that commemorates the inauguration of the temple. This festival is conducted in honor of His Holiness Sri Sri Sri Tridandi Chinna Srimannarayana Jeeyar Swamiji, under whose guidance the temple administration functions. The festival includes elaborate rituals, special pujas, cultural programs, and spiritual discourses, creating a deeply devotional and festive atmosphere.

Go Seva

GO SAMRAKSHANA SARVA JAGATH RAKSHANA

Go Seva is an integral part of Indian culture, emphasizing the service and protection of cows, which are considered sacred. It involves various practices such as Go:puja (worship), Go:da:nam (donation), and Go:raksha (protection) to preserve and nurture cows.

Go Samrakshana emphasizes the importance of preserving cows through community involvement and awareness. Initiatives include providing medical care, maintaining shelters (Go:shalas), and ensuring the well-being of indigenous breeds through sustainable practices.
